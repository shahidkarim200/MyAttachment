The Scale

The Attachment Style Questionnaire (ASQ) was adopted from Polek, E. (2008). Attachment in cultural context: Differences in attachment between Eastern and Western Europeans carried out in University of Groningen, Faculty of Behavioural and Social; which was further adopted from a Dutch study on Attachment Style by Van Oudenhoven, J. P., Hofstra, J., & Bakker, W. (2003).

Polek & E had translated the original Dutch questionnaire into English and modified the questionnaire; altering questions or removing due to avoid irrelevancy.

The ASQ is based on the Four-Factor Model of Attachment, also known as the two dimensional model of Attachment, which states that there are 4 styles of attachment namely; secure, preoccupied, dismissing, and fearful.

To learn more about the Attachment Styles, please refer to "Attachment Styles".

We then tested the ASQ for internal consistency (Cronbach's alpha) on a sample size of 122 individuals with diverse demographics pertaining to age, gender, ethnicity. In statistics and research, internal consistency is typically a measure based on the correlations between different items on the same test (or the same subscale on a larger test). It measures whether several items that propose to measure the same general construct produce similar scores (Wikipedia).

Cronbach's Alpha was developed by Lee Cronbach in 1951 to provide a measure of the internal consistency of a test or scale.
